## Question

What do you remember about the journey to reach Asfandiar in the mountains?

## Answer

When I reached Asfandiar on the other side of the mountain of Goizha, he was waiting for me with six or seven other Peshmerga. They all had Kalashnikovs, and when he saw me, oh my gosh, it was something. I hugged him; actually, I hugged his legs and I fell. He helped me up and I stopped crying. I was so anxious, tired, hungry, and very, very thirsty.

He went to a stream—it was more like a ditch with dirty, muddy water—and brought some for me. I didn't care; I just drank it because I was so thirsty. It felt like it wasn't real. 

It was dark, around one o'clock in the morning. He knew somebody was coming, but he didn't know it was me. He had a flashlight and turned it on to see who it was. The Peshmerga were yelling at him, "Don't, don't, Doctor!" they said, "The planes will see the light and then they will bomb us."
