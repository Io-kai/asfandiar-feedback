## Answer

I pretended to be a maid because my friend told me to do that; she said they would kill me if they knew who I was. My husband was a medical director in Sulaymaniyah Hospital and he was so famous; and very welknown everybody knew him. He was helping all the Kurds and Peshmerga by treating them secretly. He had an office or a clinic after hours. He never charged any poor people or people he knew because he wanted to help them because they were Kurds. In return, since those people didn't have money to pay (even though he didn't charge them), they would bring him live turkeys, live goats, or lambs. Sometimes they were so poor that I felt so bad; they would bring some kind of vegetable, like a bunch or two of parsley.

What happened was, she told me to wear an abaya. I put it on, and it didn't even look like me. We went out together through this big yard (we have a huge house together). When we opened the door to go out, one of the soldiers asked me, "Who are you?" 

I said, "I am a maid. I just cleaned up and I'm done, so I am going home." 

God saved me. I was saved that time. I did it.
