## Question

What was the hardest part of the first year in America?

## Answer

The hardest part when we arrived in America—while it was nice to be an American and free—was that it was very hard. 

I didn't know the language, and we didn't have a place to go to. It was also incredibly difficult being by ourselves with no family and nobody else around. Although my sister was here, it was still very lonely. 

We also had no jobs. My husband had to study again in medical school to get the license to work in the United States. He volunteered in Buffalo, New York, where our friend Dr. Ahmed Othman was, and he found my husband a volunteer job in the hospital. That was a very, very hard time for us.
